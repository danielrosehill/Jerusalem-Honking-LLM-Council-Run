# Council Questions

**Author:** Daniel Rosehill
**Date:** 23/04/2026
**Context:** See `problem-statement.md` — unnecessary car-horn noise pollution in Jerusalem, and the grassroots/municipal campaign to address it.

These are the six questions posed to the Policy Ideation Council. Each councillor is expected to provide one structured response **per question**, from their own policy lens, with explicit awareness of the Middle East / Israeli municipal context.

---

## Q1. Messaging strategy

What would be the most effective messaging strategies for a campaign against unnecessary car-horn use in Jerusalem? Consider audience segmentation (drivers, residents, municipal officials, police), tone, framing (public health vs. quality of life vs. environmental), and the channels most likely to reach each audience.

## Q2. Levers for municipal engagement

What are the practical "levers" the campaign should try pulling to engage local government productively — and, critically, to ensure that announced pilot projects do not quietly disappear after the initial press cycle? What mechanisms (working groups, KPIs, FOI requests, budget earmarks, inter-departmental champions, council motions) create accountability and continuity?

## Q3. Building public awareness and support

How should the campaign build broad public awareness and grassroots support, given that the issue is unevenly experienced — residents in heavily affected areas feel it acutely while others are largely indifferent? How do we convert latent frustration into organised support without alienating drivers?

## Q4. What NOT to do

What are the main pitfalls and anti-patterns to avoid when lobbying on this issue? Where have comparable advocacy campaigns damaged their own cause (e.g. overreach on surveillance, stigmatising specific communities, alienating drivers, over-reliance on flyering, confrontation in the street)?

## Q5. Comparative success stories

Are you aware of success stories from organisations or municipalities around the world — particularly from contexts with somewhat analogous driving cultures or dense mixed-use city centres — that could inform strategy? Name specific cities, programs, or NGOs, and extract the transferable mechanism (not just the headline).

## Q6. Ready-to-use snippets

Provide a handful of ready-to-use talking points, stat-anchored snippets, and short framings that campaigners can deploy in municipal meetings, press pitches, and social content. Where possible, cite a statistic (WHO noise guidelines, health impact data, enforcement ratios, etc.) that a campaigner could verify and quote.

---

## Expected output format

Each councillor returns a single JSON object with six keys (`q1` through `q6`), each containing the councillor's answer to that question from their specific policy lens. Answers should be concrete, policy-actionable, and grounded in the Middle East / Israeli municipal context where relevant.
