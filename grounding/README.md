## Key figures and facts extractable from the above

- **Current statutory fine:** 475 NIS (Traffic Ordinance / national) — recent local by-law proposals discuss 250 NIS (individual) and 500 NIS (corporation) under the Environmental Enforcement Law.
- **Israeli-wide FOI baseline (Daniel's own FOI):** ~1 ticket per day nationwide — i.e. ~300–400/year total.
- **MoEP + Israel Police joint enforcement push (2020):** 50 tickets in targeted ops in Jerusalem + Holon combined.
- **Petah Tikva pilot:** ~500 tickets in the first weeks of the NOize deployment.
- **MoEP funding envelope:** up to 10M NIS per city (≥50k residents), 70% handling commitment required.
- **Most recent Jerusalem status (Jan 2026):** 1M NIS awarded; 8 NOize-type systems deploying ~May 2026; 250 NIS fine; 2-month pre-deployment awareness campaign pledged by mayor.
- **Enabling statute:** Abatement of Nuisances (Prevention of Noise) Regulations, 5753-1992, §2(b)(1).
- **Health evidence commonly cited:** ~half the Israeli population exposed to >60 dB environmental noise; links to hypertension and cardiovascular disease.
